About

The Lantern Festival is a Chinese festival celebrated on the fifteenth day of the first month in the lunisolar Chinese calendar. It marks the final day of the traditional Chinese New Year celebrations, and usually falls on some day in February or March in the Gregorian calendar.

The first month of the lunisolar calendar is called the yuan month, and in olden times night was called xiao in Mandarin. Therefore, the day is called Yuan Xiao（元宵） Festival in China. The fifteenth day is the first full moon of that lunisolar year.

Yuanxiao vs. Tangyuan

Do you EAT typical food for celebrating the Lantern Festival like Tangyuan(汤圆) and Yuanxiao(元宵)? These are all shape like a ball, and are all white. So many people thought that it's just the same.

So do you know the difference between Tangyuan and Yuanxiao? Check the videos below.

Most Northerners eat and make Yuanxiao. Yuanxiao are made by placing balls of filling into a basket filled with glutinous rice flour, and then shaking the basket while sprinking water over it so the flour becomes a coating.

Most Southerners eat and make Tangyuan. Tangyuan, on the other hand, are made by starting with balls of rice flour dough and inserting the filling into these. In the south, salty fillings are preferred.
